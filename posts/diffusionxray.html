DiffusionXRay

Aryan Goyal • DEMI Workshop @ MICCAI 2025

A diffusion + GAN-based approach for enhancing digitally reconstructed chest radiographs, designed to preserve subtle, clinically critical details.

Back Paper Code

Motivation

Many enhancement/super-resolution pipelines rely on unrealistic paired training data (e.g., bicubic degradation), which can wash out subtle findings. We reformulate the problem to learn realistic low→high quality mappings for chest X-rays.

Method

A two-stage pipeline: (1) unsupervised style transfer to generate realistic paired low/high-quality X-rays, then (2) a conditional diffusion model trained on these pairs to produce high-quality reconstructions.

Key design choices

  • Realistic pairing: learn the degradation distribution instead of assuming bicubic.

  • Conditional DDPM: preserves fine-grained, clinically relevant details.

Results

Improved PSNR from 20.08→27.50 and SSIM from 0.83→0.92 on ChestX-ray8; radiologist evaluation reported improved visibility/preservation of subtle nodules.
